Maple Five Ways

An elegant plated dessert showcasing maple through multiple textures and techniques: cake, custard, cream, crisp tuile, and glaze.

Main Recipe

Ingredients

Maple Brown Butter Financier

  • 170 g unsalted butter
  • 90 g maple sugar
  • 60 g all-purpose flour
  • 60 g almond flour
  • 1 g kosher salt
  • 180 g egg whites
  • 30 g Grade A Dark maple syrup

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Brown the butter until golden and nutty. Cool slightly.
  3. Whisk together maple sugar, flour, almond flour, and salt.
  4. Stir in egg whites until combined.
  5. Add maple syrup and browned butter.
  6. Rest batter 15 minutes.
  7. Portion into silicone financier molds.
  8. Bake 14–18 minutes until golden brown.
  9. Cool completely before plating.

Maple Crémeux

Ingredients

  • 250 g heavy cream
  • 125 g whole milk
  • 120 g Grade A Dark maple syrup
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 20 g maple sugar
  • 2 g kosher salt
  • 3 g powdered gelatin
  • 18 g cold water

Steps

  1. Bloom gelatin in cold water.
  2. Heat cream, milk, maple syrup, and salt until steaming.
  3. Whisk yolks and maple sugar together.
  4. Temper yolks with hot cream mixture.
  5. Return to heat and cook to 180°F (82°C).
  6. Remove from heat and whisk in bloomed gelatin.
  7. Strain through a fine sieve.
  8. Chill overnight until set.
  9. Transfer to a piping bag before service.

Maple Chantilly

Ingredients

  • 240 g heavy cream
  • 30 g maple sugar
  • 15 g Grade A Dark maple syrup

Steps

  1. Chill bowl and whisk.
  2. Combine all ingredients.
  3. Whip to medium peaks.
  4. Hold refrigerated until needed.

Maple Tuile

Ingredients

  • 60 g maple syrup
  • 30 g unsalted butter, melted
  • 30 g powdered sugar
  • 30 g all-purpose flour
  • 1 egg white
  • Pinch kosher salt

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. Whisk all ingredients until smooth.
  3. Spread thin circles on a silicone baking mat.
  4. Bake 6–8 minutes until golden.
  5. Immediately shape over a rolling pin or curved mold.
  6. Allow to cool completely until crisp.

Warm Maple Glaze

Ingredients

  • 180 g Grade A Dark maple syrup
  • 28 g unsalted butter
  • Pinch kosher salt

Steps

  1. Combine maple syrup and butter in a saucepan.
  2. Heat gently until butter melts.
  3. Add salt.
  4. Hold warm for service.

Assembly

  1. Place a small pool of warm maple glaze slightly off-center on the plate.
  2. Set a maple financier partially on the glaze.
  3. Pipe or quenelle maple crémeux beside the financier.
  4. Add a rosette of maple chantilly.
  5. Insert a curved maple tuile vertically for height and visual impact.
  6. Spoon a few drops of warm glaze around the plate.
  7. Finish with a few crystals of flaky sea salt.
  8. Serve immediately.

Notes

  • Use Grade A Dark Robust maple syrup throughout for maximum maple flavor.
  • Maple sugar is critical for reinforcing maple flavor without adding excess moisture.
  • Avoid vanilla, cinnamon, bourbon, or other strong flavors that compete with maple.
  • The dessert is designed to showcase maple through five distinct textures and temperatures.
  • For competition plating, focus on clean lines, height, and negative space.
  • A warm financier paired with chilled crémeux provides excellent contrast.
