# Nile Jewel Relish with Orange Blossom

## Main Recipe

### Ingredients

- 1/2 cup Medjool dates, finely diced
- 3/4 cup pomegranate arils
- 2 tbsp dried hibiscus flowers
- 2 tbsp minced shallot
- 1 tbsp tamarind concentrate
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 1/4 tsp orange blossom water
- 2 tbsp fresh parsley, finely chopped
- 1 tsp fresh mint, finely chopped
- 1/2 tsp ground cumin
- 1/4 tsp ground coriander
- 1/4 tsp kosher salt

### Steps

1. Place the dried hibiscus in a small bowl and cover with hot water.
2. Let steep for 10 minutes, then drain thoroughly and finely chop.
3. In a mixing bowl, combine the dates, chopped hibiscus, shallot, tamarind concentrate, lemon juice, orange blossom water, cumin, coriander, and salt.
4. Mix thoroughly and allow the mixture to rest for 20 minutes.
5. Fold in the parsley, mint, and pomegranate arils just before serving.
6. Taste carefully. The orange blossom should be subtle and aromatic, not dominant.

---

## Pistachio Dukkah

### Ingredients

- 2 tbsp pistachios
- 1 tsp sesame seeds
- 1/2 tsp coriander seed
- 1/2 tsp cumin seed

### Steps

1. Toast the pistachios, sesame seeds, coriander seed, and cumin seed in a dry skillet until fragrant.
2. Allow to cool completely.
3. Crush into a coarse mixture.

---

## Assembly

1. Transfer the relish to a serving dish.
2. Sprinkle with the pistachio dukkah.
3. Garnish with a few pomegranate arils and a small mint leaf if desired.

---

## Notes

- Use orange blossom water sparingly; it should create intrigue rather than announce itself.
- This version has a more pronounced Egyptian and Eastern Mediterranean character.
- The floral aroma enhances the hibiscus and citrus while supporting the fruit.
- If making ahead for competition, consider reducing the mint to 1/2 teaspoon and increasing the parsley to maintain freshness after overnight refrigeration.
- Judges often remember aromas as much as flavors; this version is designed to create that effect.
