# Monday Red Hummus

A New Orleans-inspired hummus celebrating the tradition of Monday Red Beans and Rice.

## Main Recipe

### Ingredients

- 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 1 can (15 oz) red kidney beans, drained and rinsed
- 1/3 cup tahini
- 3 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- 1 tbsp Creole mustard
- 1/2 tsp Better Than Bouillon Ham Base
- 2 cloves roasted garlic
- 1/4 cup roasted onion
- 3 tbsp roasted celery
- 2 tbsp roasted green bell pepper
- 1 tbsp sliced green onion
- 1/2 tsp Louisiana-style hot sauce
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 3-5 tbsp ice water

### Steps

1. Roast the onion, celery, bell pepper, and garlic until softened and lightly caramelized.
2. Add the chickpeas, red beans, tahini, lemon juice, olive oil, Creole mustard, ham base, roasted vegetables, green onion, hot sauce, paprika, cayenne, and salt to a food processor.
3. Process until smooth.
4. Add ice water one tablespoon at a time until the hummus becomes light, creamy, and spreadable.
5. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.

---

## Crispy Rice Garnish

### Ingredients

- 1/2 cup cooked white rice
- 1 tsp olive oil
- Pinch of kosher salt

### Steps

1. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat.
2. Toss the rice with olive oil and salt.
3. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the rice becomes golden and crisp.
4. Transfer to a plate and cool completely.

---

## Pepper Oil

### Ingredients

- 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- 1/2 tsp Louisiana-style hot sauce
- Pinch of smoked paprika

### Steps

1. Stir the olive oil, hot sauce, and paprika together until combined.
2. Reserve for assembly.

---

## Assembly

1. Spread the hummus into a shallow serving bowl.
2. Drizzle with the pepper oil.
3. Sprinkle with crispy rice.
4. Garnish with sliced green onions.
5. Finish with a light dusting of smoked paprika.

---

## Notes

- The combination of chickpeas and red kidney beans preserves the creamy texture expected of hummus while capturing the flavor of New Orleans red beans.
- The roasted holy trinity vegetables (onion, celery, and bell pepper) provide authentic regional character.
- Better Than Bouillon Ham Base adds the savory depth traditionally associated with red beans and rice.
- Crispy rice serves as a visual and textural nod to the classic dish without altering the hummus itself.
- For competition presentation, serve in a white bowl to highlight the distinctive reddish color of the hummus.
