# Silk Road Fruit Dip

A competition-style fruit dip that blends Western creaminess with the subtle umami of white miso and the fruity complexity of aged balsamic vinegar. Designed to hold well under refrigeration for judging the next day.

## Main Recipe

### Ingredients

- 8 oz cream cheese, softened
- 4 oz mascarpone cheese
- 2 tsp white miso
- 2 Tbsp honey
- 2 tsp aged balsamic vinegar
- 1/4 tsp vanilla extract
- Pinch of salt

### Steps

1. Beat the cream cheese until completely smooth.
2. Add the mascarpone and mix until fully incorporated.
3. Add the white miso, honey, balsamic vinegar, vanilla, and salt.
4. Mix until silky and uniform.
5. Transfer to a serving container and cover.
6. Refrigerate overnight to allow the flavors to meld.
7. Stir gently before plating for judging.

---

## Suggested Fruit Pairing

### Ingredients

- Fresh strawberries
- Honeycrisp apple slices
- Pear slices
- Peach wedges (when in season)

### Steps

1. Wash and prepare fruit shortly before serving.
2. Arrange fruit around the dip or serve separately according to competition rules.

---

## Assembly

1. Transfer the chilled dip to a clean serving dish.
2. Smooth the top with a spatula.
3. Optionally drizzle with a very light amount of aged balsamic just before presentation.
4. Serve chilled with prepared fruit.

---

## Notes

- Designed specifically to improve after 12–24 hours of refrigeration.
- White miso adds depth and richness without an obvious savory flavor.
- Mascarpone provides a more stable texture than Greek yogurt during extended refrigeration.
- Use a high-quality aged balsamic vinegar rather than a balsamic glaze.
- The goal is subtle complexity, not an overtly savory fruit dip.
- Taste after overnight refrigeration and adjust honey or balsamic in future batches if needed.
